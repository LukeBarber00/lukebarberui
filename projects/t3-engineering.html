T3 Engineering

Project Overview

T3 Engineering needed a professional, scalable brand identity that reads cleanly in industrial contexts and across channels—truck vinyl, hard‑hat decals, plansets, and web. I created a modular logo system, color spec, and a set of components that are easy for non‑designers to deploy.

Research

I reviewed competitor marks in civil/MEP and construction to map legibility at distance, stroke weights for embroidery, and color contrasts suitable for safety‑orange environments. Constraints: avoid cliché gear icons, ensure the mark grids to a square for signage, and maintain contrast on cream and charcoal backgrounds.

Logo Exploration

I explored monograms built from three T's and a structural cross‑brace motif. The final icon aligns to a simple grid and holds up at 16px favicon size and 48" truck door scale.

Design

The system specifies charcoal / cream / signal‑orange with WCAG‑friendly pairs. Typography prioritizes engineering clarity over style. Assets ship as SVG and PDF with cut‑ready variants.

Final Work

Takeaway

Engineering brands win on clarity and repeatability. A tight spec—grid, color, and file kit—let this identity scale from apparel to plan stamps without custom work each time. The result: fast production, fewer errors, consistent trust.
